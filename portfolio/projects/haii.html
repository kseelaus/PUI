AI for Mental Health Intervention

An exploration of mental models for artificial intelligence as a predictor of risk for mental illness

Problem

Signs for Psychosis and other serious mental illnesses are often identifiable during adolescence, but these young people are often resistant to recieving treatment.

Solution

In a cross-disciplinary collaboration between researchers, therapists, and local theater makers, we designed an intervention to be approachable and stigma-free for teens and young adults by icorperating aspects of Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT) into an acting and improvisation class.

We recruited a group of teens and young adults identified to be at incresed risk, tested this class with two cohorts, then re-visited and improved the curriculum

Contribution

Lead team in planning and execution of the research study, collected and managed data, and recruited and coordinated participants.
