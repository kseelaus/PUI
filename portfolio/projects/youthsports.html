Youth Sports Communication

Research and design of a website for mobile and desktop to improve information flow for a youth soccer league

Challenge

Perform ethnographic research to find an area of potential improvement for a youth sports league, and design a responsive website accessible on multiple device types.

Solution

We performed guerilla research, created stakeholder personas, then designed a communication and information access portal for desktop and mobile devices.

Context

Final Project for Interaction Design Studio, Carnegie Mellon University, Fall 2018

Guerilla Research

We conducted interviews with eight volunteers who either played sports previously, were parents of child athletes, or coached a youth team at one point. Participants were found by reaching out to personal contacts and approaching parents at a local soccer game.

We learned that there are already a number of electronic resources, but many users still expressed concerns about communication, updates, and being able to check logistics.

Personas

Having learned a bit about the problem space, we created three stakeholder personas: Mike, a young soccer coach; Ellis and Talia, parents with a daughter on Mike's team; Connor, the league administrator
